Album art is an important part of the music streaming experience since it is prominently featured on a listenerâ€™s applications while streaming. However research related to streaming services has focused on user behavior, social networks, and music recommendations while album art has been under-represented. Our project offers a tool for users to visually explore their favorite album art using similarity graphs and to create their own covers using a variational autoencoder (VAE). Users will be able to explore a graph network of albums in a dataset connected through Last.FM, a popular database used by applications like Pandora and Spotify. Individual genres can then be chosen and explored further. Using a set of input albums, the variational auto-encoder will generate a composite album cover. As such, the desired functionality is twofold. First, users will be able to visually explore music, and better understand the similarity relationships between albums and musical genres. Secondly, users will be able to generate interesting cover art based on album selections.
